My Plans For My Honda Civic

My 2014 Honda Civic Si is very special to me. Once I have my first coding job, I'm going to install a bunch of parts!

Background

My first car was a 2007 Mazda 3 in manual. I had bought it six months after I passed my drivers test, and I didn’t even know how to drive stick yet. I took one lesson, and fell in love with the car, jumping into the process head first. Unfortunately, I was young and dumb so the car had issues(rod knock) that made it financially stupid to keep the car.

I sold that car and then went to the dealer to get my 2014 Honda Civic! This thing became my baby. Drove great, sounded great, treated me great. It was perfect. I’ve always wanted to mod it but never had the funds. Now that I’ve moved back to the city, I had planned to get a job and finally start giving it the parts that it deserved. Unfortunately, plans change, and so she’s going to have to wait another few months, but in the mean time I’ve bought a few parts and have planned out what I’m buying next!

Plan

My plan is to go full bolt on(FBO). This means I’ll be installing parts that are easy to install to gain more power, sound, or better feel. These are parts that even if I wanted to, I could install myself. I want my car to be as reliable as it has been to me over our time together. Eventually, when I have another car and I don’t drive this one as much, I would like to install a supercharger. Maximizing power for the car, but losing its renowned reliability.

Parts I Have Already Bought

    D2 Lowering Springs

    Used to lower the car two inches, giving it stiffer feel providing stronger support around corners while reducing the wheel gap.

    Short Ram Intake

    A three inch wide pipe flowing more air into the engine

    RV6 Catted Downpipe

    Connecting the engine to the catback exhaust, it allows the engine to exhale the exhaust fumes more efficiently

    Invidia Q300 Catback Exhaust

    Providing better airflow, it also provides a deeper exhaust tone, making high revs much more appealing

Parts I Need To Buy

    KTuner V1.2

    The absolute essential for modding any car, a tuning device. I had the V2, but realized I had no need for the large screen so I'm opting for the screenless version

    Truhart Sport Shocks

    Paired with the lowering springs, the suspension will be stiff, while also able to comfortably handle the pothole ridden New York City roads

Installation

Finally, after each part purchased, I will be taking the car up to my mechanic, Chris. He has been working on my car over my past two years of ownership. He will be installing everything in the same day, and then finally I will get the car tuned at a dyno and then she'll be good to go!
